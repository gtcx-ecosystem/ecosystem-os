## Partner Thesis

The strongest partner story is not "AI over commodity documents." It is an operating room for trusted commodity-market launch and scale.

The wedge is credible when the following pieces are presented together:

| Layer | Partner value | Primary repo spine |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Market infrastructure | Makes commodity programmes legible, repeatable, and comparable. | `markets-os`, `gtcx-os` |
| Compliance and assurance | Gives banks, DFIs, regulators, and buyers proof paths instead of informal claims. | `compliance-os`, `veritas-ai` |
| Field intelligence | Supports local language, operator support, producer context, and market adoption. | `griot-ai` |
| Evidence graph | Connects claims, scores, repo readiness, witnesses, and actions. | `ecosystem-os`, `bridge-os` |
| Governance | Keeps authority, claims, and approval boundaries explicit. | `canon-os`, `baseline-os` |
| Deployment substrate | Makes the market system operable beyond a strategy document. | `fabric-os` |
| Execution discipline | Turns partner commitments into owner-routed work with validation gates. | `agile-os` |

## Partner Rooms

### Bank or DFI room

Primary question:

```text
Can verified commodity activity become financeable operating evidence?
```

Narrative angle:

- Verified market activity, compliance evidence, and operating records reduce diligence friction.
- The system is useful before full-scale automation because it gives institutions an auditable coordination layer.
- Finance claims require current market, pilot, capital-formation, and compliance evidence before external use.

Evidence required before external use:

- current `markets-os` pilot or programme evidence
- current compliance evidence from `compliance-os`
- explicit finance workflow witness or partner-approved proof packet
- release-gate approval for partner-facing use

### Regulator or public-sector room

Primary question:

```text
Can market activity become transparent without freezing innovation?
```

Narrative angle:

- The operating layer creates role, authority, evidence, and approval records.
- Governance is not hidden inside an agent; `canon-os` and `baseline-os` define the control surface.
- Regulator-facing claims should avoid overclaiming automation, enforcement, or legal status.

Evidence required before external use:

- governance framework citations
- compliance and authority-class evidence
- clear boundary between operating records and legal/regulatory determinations
- human approval record for any regulator-facing artifact

### Enterprise buyer room

Primary question:

```text
Can buyers inspect sourcing confidence, supplier context, and verification evidence in one operating path?
```

Narrative angle:

- The value is lower-friction supplier confidence, proof routing, and operational coordination.
- `veritas-ai` and `griot-ai` become important because verification and field context reinforce each other.
- Buyer-facing claims need commodity-specific proof, not only fleet readiness.

Evidence required before external use:

- commodity-specific verification witness
- supplier or producer context evidence
- buyer workflow or onboarding path
- limitations and assumptions register

### Market operator room

Primary question:

```text
Can a market operator launch a trusted commodity programme faster with reusable operating infrastructure?
```

Narrative angle:

- The platform packages roles, handoffs, evidence, GTM docs, audit state, and execution routing.
- It is not a marketplace replacement; it is the operating layer that makes market infrastructure easier to coordinate and prove.
- `markets-os` and `gtcx-os` own market execution; `ecosystem-os` owns the category and evidence room.

Evidence required before external use:

- programme launch workflow
- repo ownership map
- cross-repo execution plan
- validation commands and current witnesses

### Investor room

Primary question:

```text
Where does the venture-scale defensibility come from?
```

Narrative angle:

- The moat is the governed ecosystem loop: graph, RAG, MCP, SIGNAL/MPR, evidence, and execution routing.
- The venture argument should separate infrastructure value from product value so driver repos do not crowd out market-facing products.
- Claims about traction, revenue, customers, and financing must remain evidence gaps until linked to current repo or partner evidence.

Evidence required before external use:

- venture scorecard and evidence gaps
- market-facing repo narrative
- deployment and operational proof
- current partner, revenue, or pilot evidence if such claims are used
