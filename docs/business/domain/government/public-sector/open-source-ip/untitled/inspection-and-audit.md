# Inspection & Audit

Continuous oversight ensures adherence to these protocols. The audit framework includes:

- Government Oversight: In Ghana, the Minerals Commission and Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA/Customs) have statutory rights to inspect mining operations and export shipments. Ghana’s AML regulators (e.g. Financial Intelligence Centre) and the Bank of Ghana may also request records under anti-money-laundering laws. In Dubai, the DMCC Authority and UAE regulators can inspect DMCC member companies and transactions.
- AGX Compliance Unit: AGX will appoint compliance officers or a committee responsible for routine checks of all records (KYC files, CoC documents, vault inventories). They conduct desk reviews of transactions and follow up on exceptions or missing files. A random sampling policy (e.g. at least 10% of shipments per quarter) triggers deeper reviews.
- Third-Party Audits: AGX will require regular audits by independent experts (e.g. LBMA/RMI accredited firms). These auditors will verify that processes (KYC, chain-of-custody logs, assay matching, vault controls) meet the standard. LBMA’s Responsible Sourcing Programme (RSP) and DMCC’s Refiner Compliance programs can be models: they use ISAE3000 audits to ensure documentation integrity.
- Physical Verifications: Unannounced inspections of gold stocks should be possible. For example, Ghana Customs or DMCC inspectors may perform random spot-checks on vault holdings, comparing physical counts to inventory. Field audits at mining sites (by the Minerals Commission or independent monitors) verify that extraction records match actual operations.
- Recordkeeping and Transparency: Suppliers are obliged to “provide access… to \[Bank of Ghana] or \[its] designated third party for inspection of relevant records”. Likewise, AGX participants must open their books to auditors. All documentation (assay certificates, receipts, manifest logs) should be kept for at least 5 years for audit trails.
- Risk Monitoring: A continuous risk-assessment process (following OECD Step 5: “report & improve”) is implemented. AGX’s compliance team tracks anomalies (e.g. sudden surges in volume from a mine, or repeated corrections in assay values) and reports them. Any transaction flagged for irregularities will halt trading and trigger a compliance review.

If any check fails (e.g. missing seals, inconsistent weights, or non-compliant KYC), the transaction is considered void and must be resolved before trade. Penalties for non-compliance should be clear: breaches may result in delisting from AGX, financial penalties, or referral to law enforcement.
