# Conclusion

AGX’s vision of a digital African gold market is innovative and potentially transformative. However, its success hinges on addressing foundational issues: ensuring legal backing and alignment with governments, building ironclad custody and audit mechanisms, and solving the underlying problem of illicit gold flows. By learning from models like Paxos (strict trust and regulation) and DMCC (government partnership), AGX can mitigate many risks. The major red flags – source verification, governance, and regulatory compliance – demand rigorous solutions. With careful design (e.g. unique batch codes, third-party audits, multi-stakeholder governance) and phased implementation, AGX can strengthen its model. If GTCX can navigate these challenges, it could indeed modernize African gold trade, but only if it remains vigilant against the very threats (fraud, smuggling, technological risk) that motivated its creation.
