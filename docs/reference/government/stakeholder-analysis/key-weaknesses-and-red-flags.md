# Key Weaknesses & Red Flags

- Source Verification Risk: If even well-certified systems in Brazil failed, AGX’s traceability is only as strong as its weakest link. Without physical verification (like nano tags or chemical assays) and rigorous audits, false data can enter the chain undetected. This is a critical red flag. Strengthening: Incorporate multi-factor verification (e.g. GPS-logged mining-site sampling, third-party assay results) rather than simple self-reporting.<br>
- Regulatory Uncertainty: The legal status of tokenized commodities is still fluid. A crackdown on crypto-assets in any key African country or trading partner could freeze AGX. Strengthening: Engage regulators now; possibly build AGX as a consortium (not just a private startup) to reduce the chance of being labeled rogue.<br>
- Dependency on IT: AGX’s platform is only as resilient as its tech. A smart contract bug or malicious attack could halt all operations. Strengthening: Rigorous third-party audits of code, plus an “upgrade” path if flaws are found. Consider insurance or guarantees to users for technical failures.<br>
- Corruption & Smuggling: Unless AGX ties into border control and real-world law enforcement, illicit gold may simply bypass the system. Strengthening: Work with initiatives like Ghana’s anti-smuggling task force or international efforts (OECD due diligence guidelines). Public-private partnerships (PPPs) can help – e.g., share intelligence on gold movements between AGX and governments.<br>
- Governance Gaps: If governance is opaque, trust will falter. Strengthening: Establish a transparent, multi-stakeholder governance charter before launch. Perhaps create an independent oversight body including NGOs, similar to how LBMA includes NGO observers.<br>
- Economic Incentives: If AGX fees or regulations make participation unattractive, users will avoid it. For example, requiring all transactions to be on-chain might be onerous. Strengthening: Consider off-chain order books or escrow options for high-volume trades to keep costs reasonable, while still settling on-chain for security.
