# Operational Risks

- Logistics: Secure transport of gold from mine to vault is a traditional weak point. AGX might require all gold to be sent to designated vault centers (similar to Ghana’s centralized PMMC system). This centralization improves control but adds cost/time. In remote regions, AGX could partner with armored transport or local banks. Insurance premiums may be high, inflating costs. AGX should stagger roll-out, perhaps starting in regions with good infrastructure (e.g. Ghana, South Africa) before remote zones.
- Infrastructure: Digital token trading requires reliable internet and power. Many mining areas lack connectivity. AGX must design for offline fallback: miners could log batches on local systems and upload when online. Alternatively, AGX might use mobile-based registration at collecting centers (similar to e-vouchers in other commodity trades). A single blockchain node outage shouldn’t halt transactions; using a permissioned consortium blockchain with multiple mirrors (in different countries) can provide redundancy.
- Refiner Collusion: Smugglers often corrupt refiners. If African gold is exported to Dubai or Switzerland for refining, AGX cannot track it afterward. This risk cannot be solved by AGX alone. Mitigation: foster domestic refining or ensure AGX tokens can only be redeemed in African jurisdictions. Promote on-continental refining (e.g. Ghana plans to refine more gold) so that the chain never exits AGX’s purview.
- Commodity Volatility: Gold prices fluctuate; if tokens fall in value, miners may be left with depreciated payouts. AGX might offer advance contracts or hedging tools to miners. On the flip side, token volatility invites speculative trading; AGX should enforce that tokens strictly represent underlying gold (no fractional reserve).
- Tech Failure: A blockchain node bug or cryptographic breach could undermine trust. AGX should adopt well-tested blockchain platforms (e.g. those audited by KPMG or Finra) and conduct formal security audits. A contingency plan (like moving to a new chain) should be pre-approved.
