# FP5: Trust Through Transparency

---

### `FP5` Trust Through Transparency

Legitimate transactions should not require opacity for protection. GTCX creates trust through verifiable transparency rather than confidential authority, enabling participants to prove rather than claim their compliance and quality.

Transparency operates at multiple levels within GTCX systems. Compliance scoring methodologies are publicly documented, enabling participants to understand precisely how scores are calculated and what actions improve their ratings. This creates learning systems where transparency drives improvement rather than gaming or manipulation.

Privacy protection and transparency coexist through careful system design. Sensitive commercial information remains confidential while compliance verification becomes publicly verifiable. Zero-knowledge proofs enable parties to demonstrate adherence to standards without revealing underlying data, protecting competitive information while building market trust.

Audit trails provide complete transaction history without compromising operational security. Every custody transfer, compliance assessment, and settlement transaction generates cryptographically signed records that can be independently verified but cannot be altered or falsified. This creates accountability without surveillance, verification without violation of privacy.

Transparent governance ensures that protocol evolution serves participant interests rather than developer preferences. All significant decisions undergo public consultation, documented reasoning, and open review processes. The infrastructure serves its users because its users maintain visibility and voice in its development and operation.
