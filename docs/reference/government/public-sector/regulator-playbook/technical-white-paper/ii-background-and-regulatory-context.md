# II — Background & Regulatory Context

**Section II — Background & Regulatory Context**

> **Objective:** Offer regulators, policy analysts, and industry partners a concise yet comprehensive overview of the historical backdrop, present‑day pressures, and impending regulatory horizons that frame the need for the Global Trust & Compliance Exchange (GTCX).

---

### `1.0` Historical Regulatory Challenges

#### `1.1` Legacy Oversight Models (Pre‑2010)

- <mark style="background-color:red;">**Paper‑Centric Processes:**</mark> Licenses, assay results, and export clearances primarily processed via paper forms, introducing delays and fraud opportunities.
- <mark style="background-color:red;">**Siloed Databases:**</mark> Customs, mining ministries, and fiscal authorities maintained separate, non‑interoperable systems, limiting end‑to‑end traceability.
- <mark style="background-color:red;">**Reactive Enforcement:**</mark> Inspections and audits often based on periodic sampling rather than continuous monitoring, resulting in low detection rates.

### `2.0` Contemporary Regulatory Pressures (2019‑2025)

#### `2.1` Global ESG & Human‑Rights Mandates

- **OECD DDG Expansion:** 2021 update emphasised downstream refiner responsibility for upstream risk assessments.
- **EU Conflict Minerals Regulation:** Came into force 2021, requiring importers to exercise due‑diligence on 3Ts & gold.
- **US Uyghur Forced Labour Prevention Act (UFLPA):** 2022 law placing onus on importers to prove goods are not linked to forced labour.

#### `2.2` Climate & Sustainability Reporting

- **EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM):** Phased reporting (2023‑2026) incentivises carbon‑intensity disclosure.
- **EU Battery Regulation (2023/1542):** Mandates Digital Product Passport for critical minerals by 2027.
