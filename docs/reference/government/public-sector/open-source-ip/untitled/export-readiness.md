# Export Readiness

Before any export from Ghana or Dubai, a strict checklist of documents and certifications must be completed. The key steps, based on Ghana’s Minerals Commission and PMMC procedures, are:

1. Export License & Notification: The exporter (LGE) must hold a valid export license (from Ghana Gold Board/PMMC or DMCC). At least two business days before each shipment, the LGE notifies PMMC in writing of its export plan.
2. Assaying: The gold consignment is delivered to a designated PMMC assay center. Along with the gold, submit: Packing List, Commercial Invoice, Ghana Customs export declaration form, and any export permit. PMMC will assay the gold to determine content and purity using an agreed-upon method. The supplier pays the standard assay fee (0.1% of value).
3. Certificate of Analysis: PMMC issues a certified Report of Analysis of the gold. Copies go to the Bank of Ghana, Customs, and Minerals Commission. This document is the official proof of metal content.
4. Inspection and Sealing: A Ghana Customs Officer inspects the assayed gold. The officer supervises placement of the Customs Division’s seal on the gold containers or bars. The customs official signs the export documents, confirming the quantity and weights.
5. Port Clearance: At Kotoka Intl. Airport or other exit point, the LGE completes any remaining GRA export formalities. The sealed gold is loaded onto the aircraft under Customs supervision.
6. Legal Conditions: Exports are not valid without the dual seals and full paperwork. By law, “Gold ore cannot be exported… without the seals of the Customs Division and the PMMC. Full documentation must accompany the export.”. The AGX system will only recognize exports that have this certification.
7. Post-Export Reporting: After each shipment, the LGE must submit a report to the Minerals Commission on exported quantities (as per licence conditions). These monthly returns enable audits and ensure alignment between reported and actual shipments.

In Dubai (DMCC), analogous documentation is required. Refined bars should carry DMCC Good Delivery or LBMA Good Delivery status, with assay certificates from accredited labs. All import/export declarations must align with UAE customs requirements. AGX will maintain templates of required forms (Export/Import Permits, Assay certificates, commercial invoices, etc.) and mandate pre-export submission in the compliance portal.
