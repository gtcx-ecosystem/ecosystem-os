# Vault & Custody

All gold in transit or storage must be held in secure, accredited vaults that meet international best practices. Vault standards include:

- Physical Security: Multi-layered security (reinforced walls, controlled access, motion detectors, armed guards). Vault design should follow LBMA best practices: for example, LBMA advises choosing secure locations (near support police), certified vault construction, and state-of-the-art alarm/CCTV systems. Entrance and storage areas should be strictly separated.
- Inventory Controls: A computerized inventory management system must track every bar’s identity, weight, and ownership. All movements (inbound receipts, outbound deliveries) are logged immediately. Each bar should carry a unique serial or RFID tag linked to a manifest. LBMA guidance notes that vaults should allow customers to audit stock regularly, and staff should “validate holdings within your facility”.
- Insurance: High-value insurance covering the full metal content is mandatory. Policies must cover theft, damage, and natural disasters. (LBMA recommends insurers review the vault’s security and inventory controls; costs vary by location.)
- Audits: Vaults are subject to periodic independent audits. These include on-site audits by certified “assurance providers” per LBMA RSP or DMCC risk framework, verifying that physical stocks match records. Staff and processes are also reviewed. AGX-approved vaults should be listed (e.g. DMCC Good Delivery vaults or LBMA-recognized custodians).
- Handovers: Transfers of gold into/out of a vault require dual-control (two authorized staff), signed receipts, and re-sealing. Any transfer between vaults (e.g. Ghana to Dubai) must use insured, sealed transport with tracking.

Vaults in Ghana (e.g. Bank of Ghana or PMMC vaults) should meet these standards. For gold moving to Dubai, DMCC’s Dubai Good Delivery (DGD) and Accredited Vault program can be used. All vault facilities must provide documentation of compliance: security plans, audit reports, insurance certificates, and staff clearance records.
