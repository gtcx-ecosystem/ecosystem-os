# Chain-of-Custody

A strict chain-of-custody (CoC) process tracks gold from extraction to export. Each transfer point must be logged with documentation and verified by authorized parties. The steps below outline the movement and the required records:

1. Mining Site (Origin): Only licensed mining operations (large-scale or ASM permitted by law) may enter AGX supply chain. At the mine, a production log must record date, quantity, and method of extraction. Mining permits, environmental permits, and titles must be presented. (For ASM, miners should use designated collection centers and only sell to registered buyers.)
2. Local Collection/Transport: Gold is packaged (e.g. in sacks or sealed bags) and transported by approved security-hired vehicles. Each load must have a trip manifest listing quantity, origin GPS coordinates, and chain-of-custody seals. At intermediate points (e.g. regional depots), weights are recorded on weighbridge tickets signed by the buyer and transporter. All handlers (e.g. buying agents) keep copies of receipts.
3. Assay and Refinement: Before export, raw gold or doré must be assayed by the Precious Minerals Marketing Company (PMMC) or an accredited assayer. The exporter submits the gold to PMMC with a Declaration Form, Packing List, and Invoice. PMMC produces an Assay Certificate detailing metal content and purity, with copies sent to Bank of Ghana, Customs, and the Minerals Commission. Bars or doré are stamped or bagged with the assayer’s mark and serial number to link with the certificate.
4. Customs Sealing: A Ghana Revenue Authority (Customs) officer inspects the assayed gold at the PMMC assay center. The officer oversees placement of official Customs seals on each container or package of gold. The Customs Declaration Form (E2) is endorsed. No gold leaves Ghana without these seals.
5. Export Documentation: The Licensed Gold Exporter (LGE) brings all paperwork (Assay Cert, export licenses, invoices, GRA forms) to Kotoka International Airport. Cargo is checked by GRA/Customs before loading. By law, “Gold ore cannot be exported by LGE without the seals of the Customs Division and the PMMC. Full documentation must accompany the export.”. The AGX registry must reflect these documents and seals in its audit trail.
6. (If Applicable) Refining & Re-export: If gold is refined or re-bagged (e.g. in a Dubai refinery), a new chain-of-custody begins: refiner assaying, new seals, and shipping docs per LBMA/DMCC rules. The final ownership transfer is logged on AGX with the refiner’s certification.

Throughout, supporting evidence (geological surveys, mine production records, origin certificates) should be kept on file. Independent traceability reports may be required to verify that gold indeed came from the stated mine of origin. AGX should audit these records: inspectors or third parties verify that assay certificates match shipment weights and that seals remain intact.
