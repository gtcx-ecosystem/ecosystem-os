# Business Model Assumptions & Flaws


## Overview

- Assumption – Willing Participants: AGX presumes licensed miners will voluntarily register batches and sell through the platform. In reality, many small-scale miners operate informally or illegally. If only licensed miners can participate, AGX may exclude much of the production (32–41% of African gold is undeclared) and fail to curb smuggling. Mitigation: AGX must work with governments (e.g. Ghana’s Gold Board approach) to register miners and mandate reporting. Offering fair pricing and guaranteed payment may incentivize miners, but enforcement (e.g. limiting legal sales to code-tagged gold) is required.
- Assumption – Trust in Digital Tokens: The model assumes buyers (retail and institutional) will trust tokens representing gold. But without strong proofs of backing, investors will be skeptical. Flaw: If token issuers do not maintain 1:1 backing in secure vaults, or if auditing is weak, tokens may be viewed as untrustworthy. Mitigation: Adopt best practices from existing models: for example, Paxos’ Pax Gold (PAXG) provides on-chain lookup of the exact gold bar backing each token; Mesh.trade’s tokens are redeemable for physical coins stored in audited Brink’s vaults. AGX should similarly ensure each token equals a specific, audited gold bar or coin, and offer verifiable proof (e.g. serial number lookup or digital certificates) to buyers.
- Assumption – Regulatory Support: AGX assumes regulators will allow tokenized gold trading. This is uncertain: many African countries have strict controls on gold exports and digital assets. Flaw: If regulators deem tokens as unlicensed securities or if international regulators block transfers, AGX liquidity suffers. Mitigation: AGX needs legal clarity: work with regulators (financial authorities, central banks) to classify tokens properly (likely as commodity-backed instruments) and comply with AML/CFT rules. For instance, Mesh.trade partnered with a registered South African bullion dealer and a licensed vault to fit local regulations. AGX should preemptively register as a regulated commodities exchange or digital asset service provider in each jurisdiction.
